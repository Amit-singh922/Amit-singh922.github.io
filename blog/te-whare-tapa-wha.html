Te Whare Tapa Whā Wellbeing Plan

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

Regular physical activity not only improves my physical health but also contributes to mental wellbeing by reducing stress and anxiety.

I go for a run or to the gym regularly to maintain good health.

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

Connecting with family strengthens social bonds and provides a sense of belonging, which is essential for overall wellbeing.

I call my family at least twice a week to maintain a healthy relationship and support my family where required.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

Meditation helps in calming the mind, reducing stress, and improving focus, thus enhancing emotional and mental wellbeing.

I do meditation early in the morning or sometimes any other time of the day in a quiet room with no noise.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

Spiritual practices like prayer and gratitude can provide a sense of purpose, meaning, and connectedness, contributing to overall spiritual wellbeing.

i set aside some time each day for prayer or expressing gratitude to be thankful what I have in my life.

Whenua (Interconnections to the Land and Environment)

Connecting with nature, such as walking barefoot on the beach, has been shown to improve mental and physical wellbeing by reducing stress and increasing feelings of relaxation and happiness.

I walk barefoot on the beach sometimes I find it very relaxing.

By incorporating these activities into my routine, I aim to strengthen all aspects of my wellbeing according to the Te Whare Tapa Whā model.
